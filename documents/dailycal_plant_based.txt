Local Events
Berkeley’s connection to progressive food movements including farm-to-table dining, organic sourcing and vegetarian cuisine, has found strong footholds around the UC Berkeley campus. For students and locals alike, the city offers a delicious array of eateries that blend flavor with sustainability. Among the standouts are Mezzo, Gather, Little Plearn, D’Yar and Cheeseboard — five restaurants that make plant-based eating genuinely exciting.
Mezzo
This classic sandwich and salad joint is located on Telegraph Avenue, serving hearty meals to generations of UC Berkeley students. After a brief closure and relocation, Mezzo continues its legacy of generous portions and customizable, vegetarian-friendly meals. Mezzo’s signature salad, piled high with marinated tofu, garbanzo beans, kidney beans and housemade vinaigrette, hits the sweet spot between nutrition and indulgence. Their focus on fresh, local produce and waste-conscious operations like compostable packaging aligns perfectly with the values of environmentally conscious eaters.
Gather
Tucked beside the David Brower Center, Gather has sustainability written all over it. The menu rotates with the seasons, drawing from organic farms across Northern California. While the sit-down restaurant is not exclusively vegetarian, Gather has signature dishes like charred brassicas with chili tahini and squash blossom pizza with almond ricotta in autumn. Dining here feels less like a meal and more like a wholesome, sustainable experience.
Little Plearn
A downtown neighborhood gem on Shattuck Avenue, Little Plearn offers cozy Thai comfort food with a vegetarian-forward approach. The menu checks the boxes for vegan cuisine and the kitchen gladly customizes dishes, such as swapping tofu for meat or steering clear of fish sauce on request. Standouts include the spicy eggplant stir-fry and the pumpkin curry, both bursting with flavor and warmth. Thai food is traditionally nonvegetarian, however Little Plearn rejects this notion, creating an innovative yet still authentic lineup.
D'Yar
Close to campus on Durant Avenue, D’Yar is a favorite for fast, flavorful Mediterranean meals. Think falafel wraps, lentil soup, tabbouleh and grilled veggie plates — all rich in fiber, protein and spice. Serving generous portions, D’Yar is a go-to for students fueling up on a budget. The menu emphasizes legumes, grains, chickpeas and vegetables, which help reduce the overall carbon footprint of their offerings.
Cheeseboard
No article about Berkeley’s sustainable food scene is complete without a Cheeseboard feature. This worker-owned cooperative serves legendary vegetarian pizzas, rotating daily with ingredients sourced from local farms. Its single “pizza of the day” model not only reduces waste but allows for seasonal creativity; imagine a delectable roasted squash pizza with lemon zest or corn pizza with poblano cream. Pro tip: Make sure to line up early! There is always a line piling out the front. Eating a slice on the patio or sidewalk while listening to their live jazz performances is not just a simple meal, it is an unforgettable culinary experience for Berkeley residents.
Plant-based eating is not just a food trend in Berkeley, it is part of a larger ethos that connects food, justice and climate. Each of these restaurants contributes to a culture that values flavor, community and ecological responsibility. Whether you are a longtime vegan or just looking to add more greens to your plate, Berkeley makes it easy — and delicious. The future of food is already cooking in Berkeley.
