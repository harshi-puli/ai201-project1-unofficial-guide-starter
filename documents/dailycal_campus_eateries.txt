Campus tour guides often point to The Golden Bear Café as the spot for grabbing a quick bite before class, but for many experienced Berkeley foodies like myself, there are better options.
As a passionate chef, I find myself constantly on the search for new meals and cuisines to try on and around campus. Whether it be at restaurants or grab-and-go spots, I always have an appetite for trying new things.
My friends consistently tell me I should switch career paths and become a food reviewer. While for the meantime that is not what my future holds, my foodie tendencies have led me to compile my own guide to eateries on campus.
Now from my mukbanger heart to yours… Here’s the best of UC Berkeley’s eateries.
For an L.A. vibe: Brown’s
The short cherry blossom tree-lined walk to the northwest part of campus is always worth the reward of Brown’s. I’ve called Los Angeles home for most of my life, and the cuisine offered at Brown’s reminds me of how most people would typically describe an L.A. brunch spot. From the breakfast avocado toast and plant-based chorizo burrito to the lunch plate with your choice of protein, greens and grains, Brown’s is always like a taste of home to me.
The overall student pick: Golden Bear Café
At around 12-3 p.m. every weekday without fail, The Golden Bear Cafe, or GBC, will be packed with French-fry hungry people. GBC offers three different food services for students: grab-and-go snacks, Peet’s (coffee and pastries) and the eatery itself. Its fries are the most notable item on the menu, alongside many other dishes.
Ideal for brunch: Local x Design
Local x Design does not take meal swipes, but I think it’s definitely worth the flex dollars to visit every once in a while. Its breakfast bagel sandwiches are packed with flavor and make any day brighter, especially if you decide to eat them right outside of Local x Design on the Wurster Hall patio. The seating area outside of Wurster Hall is a great sunny spot on warm days, but if it gets too warm during their lunch hours, Faculty Glade is close by for a shady picnic.
Overlooked but outstanding: Monsoon Kitchens
Located on the lower level of the Martin Luther King Jr. Student Union, Monsoon Kitchens offers Katora bowls which include your choice of grain, protein and various side options including naan and samosas. Its food comes in reusable containers that are collected in bright orange bins nearby. From what I’ve noticed, Monsoon Kitchens often gets overlooked by its proximity to the more popular spot, Ladle & Leaf, but it is a gem hidden in plain sight. A student favorite menu item is the chicken tikka masala, but personally, I enjoy the palak paneer.
My Go-To: Ladle & Leaf
Out of all of the on-campus eateries, I find myself consistently going back to Ladle and Leaf the most. Located down the steps next to Sproul and inside of the student union building, Ladle & Leaf offers quality soups, sandwiches, salads and bowls. Its food tastes fresh, and the flavor profiles are always distinct and well-crafted. My favorite half-salad-and-half-soup meal combination is its brussels caesar salad with the mac and cheese.
Transitioning to life on campus can be challenging at first, but with these eateries to supplement dining hall meals, there is a good variety of quality food to discover.
There are many different flavors to the Berkeley experience; make sure to savor every one.
