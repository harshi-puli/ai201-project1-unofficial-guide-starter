One of the perks of UC Berkeley’s campus and location is being surrounded by countless quality coffee shops — perfect for coffee lovers and students hoping to “lock in” in a cozy, aesthetic environment. Whether you’re craving a latte after class on Northside or are meeting a friend near Telegraph Ave., here is a guide to some of Berkeley’s top coffee and study spots, featuring the best caffeinated drinks and ambiances.
Southside Favorites
Starting off on the southside of campus, closest to the MLK Student Union, there are two must-try cafés.
Sodoi Coffee Tasting House, located at 2438 Durant Ave., offers a strong and unique coffee blend in both their lattes and espresso drinks. You can never go wrong with their iced vanilla latte, but they also serve creative specialty options, such as their red velvet latte or chaga mushroom drinks. The café has plenty of seating and outlets for long study sessions, although it can fill up quickly during peak hours. However, keep in mind that you must order a drink in order to sit inside.
Just across the street, 1951 Coffee Company at 2410 Channing Way — located next to Unit 3  — is another great stop for quality espresso drinks and matcha. If it’s your first time, make sure to try their lychee strawberry matcha or the honey lavender latte — both lightly sweetened and perfect for an afternoon pick-me-up. Like Sodoi, 1951’s indoor and outdoor seating fills up fast. Luckily, it also makes a great option to get coffee on the way to class!
Downtown Picks
Making our way to Downtown Berkeley, Café Etoile, located at 1930 Martin Luther King Jr. Way, is a great option for a longer weekend study session. Not only does Café Etoile offer a delicious selection of drinks, including unique latte flavors like tiramisu, but they also stand out for their French-Korean fusion brunch menu — from their chicken and croissant waffle, or “croffle,” to their bulgogi sandwich. This Downtown Berkeley gem has spacious seating and ample charging outlets, perfect for a Sunday brunch or study date.
On-Campus Spots
Heading over to campus, Kiklo Cafe — located inside Stanley Hall on University Drive — is a hidden gem. With both indoor and outdoor seating, natural light and strong espresso, it’s a perfect stop between classes. Don’t miss out on their Spanakopita or Greek Nefeli Salad for a hearty lunch or their London Fog for (I swear) the cheapest drink on campus!
Nearby, Yali’s Qualcomm Cafe at 2594 Hearst Ave. offers a similar vibe near the College of Engineering. It has ample seating and a reliable selection of sandwiches, paninis and pastries with direct access to a meditation room in case you’re in need of some deep silence to escape the bustle of campus life.
Elmwood
Finally, if you make your way down to the Elmwood area (a few blocks west from the Clark Kerr Campus), you will find the hidden gem Souvenir Coffee Co. at 2701 College Ave. With its large windows and comfortable seating, Souvenir is a great choice for focused work sessions. They’re open daily until 5 p.m., making it a great option for afternoon work periods.
Last but not least, you can never go wrong with Philz Coffee at 6310 College Ave. With signature drinks like the Mint Mojito and Philtered Soul, it’s perfect for those who prefer unique and customizable blends. Located near a Safeway and Trader Joe’s, Philz is convenient to complement a productive weekend grocery run.
Overall, Berkeley is full of great coffee and comfortable study spaces. While this list is most definitely not exhaustive, hopefully it can help you discover your next go-to café for studying, catching up with friends or treating yourself to some alone time.
